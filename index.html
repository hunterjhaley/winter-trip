Where this landed

Three finalists, one clear pick

The rule set hasn't changed: no thin air for Grandma (Granby's 7,935 ft is the never-again line), every age wins, and now a real number: about $30,000 all-in from the driveway in Livingston and back. Seven nights, better everything, home on a Saturday with Sunday to land.

The three, in order

  • 🥇 Option 1: Quebec CitySea level, the most magical week for the most people, and the only option where $30K buys the UPGRADED trip: lofts inside the 400-year-old walls, a splurge dinner, the ice hotel, tubing, and a real ski day at Mont-Sainte-Anne 30 minutes away. Comes in around $23K, leaving room to say yes to things. Fully built out in its tab.
  • 🥈 Option 2: Mont TremblantThe ski-village version: best kid ski school in the East, fireworks over the village at midnight on New Year's Eve, everything walkable. Lands around $26K. Real trip, real skiing, still under ceiling.
  • 🥉 Option 3: WhistlerThe world-class mountain, and the honest one: for nine people over New Year's it prices at roughly $32K, a little over the line even played smart. Included because it's the best skiing of the three and the family should see the real number, not a fantasy one.
Why Quebec City wins the money test: the other two charge resort-village prices for beds and lift access. Quebec City charges city prices, and the skiing is a day trip instead of the whole bill. Same magic, different math.

Option 1 • The pick • sea level

Quebec City, fully built

Seven nights inside a 400-year-old walled city that decorates like a movie set: toboggans, an ice hotel, tubing mountains, a real ski day, and New Year's fireworks. Every place below is real, rated, and mapped. This is the trip I'd book.

Where we stay

Two lofts inside the walls

Top pick: the home base

Les Lofts Ste-Anne

42 Rue Sainte-Anne • facing the holiday market square
★ 4.7 (76)Full kitchens + free laundryBunk beds the kids fight overUnderground parking below the square

Apartment-style lofts in a historic building literally facing the Christmas market square, with full kitchens, big living rooms, laundry, and bunk rooms. We take two lofts: one for each family cluster, Grandma gets a real bedroom, and breakfast happens in pajamas before the city wakes up. Reviewers call the host's recommendation list "essentially our itinerary." Booking two units for seven nights is the single biggest budget win of the whole trip.

❤️ Grandma factor: her own room, an elevator-free but low-floor request at booking, coffee in hand watching the market square from the window. She holds court; the city comes to her.

The shape of the week

Day by day, Play A

  • Sat Dec 26IAH to Quebec City (one stop), land afternoon, vans to the lofts, first walk under the lights, easy dinner at La Buchette two doors down.
  • Sun Dec 27Old city day: La Bûche breakfast, funicular down to Petit Champlain and Place Royale, first toboggan runs, Le Lapin Sauté dinner.
  • Mon Dec 28Valcartier day: ice hotel tour in the morning, tubing all afternoon, pizza-and-laundry night at the lofts.
  • Tue Dec 29Ski day at Mont-Sainte-Anne: lessons for the littles, big mountain for the rest, Grandma at the summit lodge. Montmorency Falls on the drive home.
  • Wed Dec 30Split day: Strøm spa for Grandma and parents, museum and skating crew with the teens, everyone back for the adults' Chez Rioux & Pettigrew night while the 18 year old runs movie night.
  • Thu Dec 31Sleep in, carriage ride at dusk, big early dinner, then New Year's Eve in the streets: music, fireworks, midnight inside the walls.
  • Fri Jan 1Slow brunch at Cochon Dingue, last toboggan runs, last shopping on Petit Champlain, pack.
  • Sat Jan 2Fly home, land IAH, drive to Livingston. Sunday belongs to the couch.
⚠️ Straight talk: Quebec in late December runs cold, sometimes single digits. The gear line in the budget exists because Texans showing up in hoodies would ruin day one. Real coats, real boots, real gloves, bought before we fly.

The Money

Every number below is a planning estimate in US dollars for all nine of us, seven nights, driveway to driveway. Peak-week fares and rooms swing, so treat these as honest centers, not quotes; once the family picks, we price the winner for real.

Option 1 • Quebec City • ~$23,000

The full breakdown

  • Flights, 9 seats: ~$7,200Houston to Quebec City round trip, one stop, holiday-week fares around $800 a seat booked by early fall. Nonstop to Montreal plus a 3 hour drive can shave $1,000+ total if fares spike; both paths priced at booking.
  • Lodging, 2 lofts, 7 nights: ~$4,800Two full-kitchen lofts inside the walls at roughly $340 a night each at holiday rates. Sleeps all nine with real beds and laundry.
  • Vans + fuel + parking: ~$1,800Two minivans for the week (airport, Valcartier day, ski day, falls), underground parking below the square, gas.
  • Food, 7 days: ~$3,200Big loft breakfasts, one grocery run, restaurant lunches and dinners from the lineup, the adults' splurge night included.
  • The ski day: ~$1,100Mont-Sainte-Anne tickets for the skiers, two kids' lessons, rentals, Grandma's gondola ride, mountain lunch for nine.
  • Ice hotel + tubing day: ~$700Nine day passes covering the Hôtel de Glace visit and the Valcartier winter playground.
  • The rest of the fun: ~$900Toboggan runs all week, skating, carriage ride, Strøm spa for three, museum, Montmorency cable car.
  • Winter gear for Texans: ~$1,200Real coats, boots, gloves, and thermals for nine before we fly. Non-negotiable and reusable.
  • Travel insurance: ~$600Nine travelers, international, holiday week. Cheap insurance against one twisted ankle.
  • Buffer: ~$1,500The line that makes every other line honest.
Total: ~$23,000~$7,000 under ceiling
The upgrade fund, if we want to spend the gap: one night in the ice hotel for the parents (~$900), the Légende tasting menu (~$800), a second ski day (~$900), Royal Dalhousie suites instead of the lofts (~$1,000 more), or it just comes home with us.

Bottom line

Quebec City delivers the most once-in-a-lifetime week per dollar and leaves $7,000 of air under the ceiling. Tremblant buys real skiing and still fits.
