The problem we're solving

Why we're not going back to Colorado

Granby sits at 7,935 feet. Grandma struggled to breathe and walk the whole week, and she has said plainly: no more high altitude. So the whole list starts from one rule.

The three ground rules

  • 1. The elevation gate comes firstEvery destination on this list sits far below Granby. Most are under 2,300 feet; the highest is still 43 percent lower than where she struggled. If a place gambles with her breathing, it drops, no matter how pretty.
  • 2. All six age groups have to winAges 4, 6, 12, 18, the parents (35 to 38), and Grandma (66). Every card below has a "for each person" section, and if one of us has nothing, it counts against the rank.
  • 3. Snow is preferred, not requiredWe love a winter trip, but great scenery, great food, and a week everyone actually enjoys beat a snow guarantee.

Ranks 1 to 3 • The push

Team Canada

Here's the honest math: three of the four best answers on this list were already Canadian before anyone lobbied. Low elevation, real mountains, easy flights from Houston, your dollar goes further, and December there looks like the inside of a snow globe.

Why Canada wins on paper

  • The elevation problem disappearsQuebec City is sea level. Tremblant's village is 875 feet. Even Whistler, a top-five ski resort on earth, bases at just 2,214 feet. Grandma breathes easy at all three.
  • Shortest international flights on the listNonstop options run Houston to Montreal (about 4 hours) and Houston to Vancouver (about 5.5), then a scenic drive. Compare that to 15-plus hours to Lapland or Japan.
  • Your dollar stretchesThe exchange rate has favored the US dollar for years, so hotels and dining run a real discount to what the sticker says.
  • It's genuinely world-class, not a consolation prizeWhistler is a former Olympic host. Quebec City is a UNESCO walled city. Tremblant throws one of the best family New Year's Eves in North America.
1
Best for everyone • Canada

Quebec City

Old Quebec, walled city • sea level to 345 ft
Elevation: noneThe whole-family winner1-stop flights, ~7 to 9 hrs door to doorSnow: near-certain

A 400-year-old walled city that turns into a Christmas card for two straight weeks: cobblestone streets under lights, a German market, horse-drawn carriages, and a toboggan run that has been screaming down beside the Château Frontenac since 1884. Nobody skis all day here; everybody wanders, eats, sleds, and stares. That's exactly why it ranks first for this crew.

✨ The once-in-a-lifetime stuff
  • The Dufferin Terrace tobogganThree-lane wooden chute beside the Château, running since 1884, up to about 40 mph, and the 4 year old can ride with a parent. The single best two dollars in Canada. Photos
  • Hôtel de GlaceNorth America's only ice hotel, rebuilt from scratch every winter just outside town. Day tours for the family; the parents can book one very cold, very unforgettable night if they're brave. Photos
  • Petit Champlain at ChristmasRoutinely called the most beautiful street in Canada, and in late December it's wall-to-wall lights, wreaths, and snow. The photo that ends up framed. Photos
  • Montmorency Falls, frozenA waterfall taller than Niagara that grows a "sugarloaf" ice cone at its base. Cable car to the top, lit at night, 15 minutes from the walls. Photos
👪 For each person
  • The 4 & 6
    Toboggan runs, carriage rides, skating at Place D'Youville, hot chocolate on repeat, and snow everywhere they look.
  • The 12
    Ice hotel tour, skating, the toboggan without a parent, crêpe stands, Petit Champlain shopping.
  • The 18
    The most photogenic city on the continent, French cafés, old-town nightlife, NYE on Grande Allée.
  • Parents
    Serious French dining, wine bars, Strom Nordic spa on the river, one ice-hotel night if they dare.
  • Grandma
    The funicular does the climbing, the old town is flat once you're up, tea at the Château Frontenac, carriage tours instead of walks. This is her best trip on the whole list.
❤️ Grandma factor: sea level, funicular instead of stairs, carriages instead of hikes, and a grand hotel lobby to hold court in. Zero asterisks.
⚠️ The catch: December cold is real here, single digits some nights, and there's no big-mountain skiing in town (small hills nearby only). This is the magic trip, not the ski trip. Pack like Texans who mean it.

2
Best real ski week • Canada

Whistler Blackcomb

British Columbia • village base 2,214 ft
Elevation: easyTop-5 ski resort on earthNonstop IAH to Vancouver ~5.5 hrs + 2 hr driveSnow: guaranteed

If the vote says "we want a real ski trip," this is the best one that exists at low altitude, full stop. Two Olympic mountains, a car-free pedestrian village, and the highest lift capacity in North America, with a base village lower than Amarillo, Texas. The Sea-to-Sky highway drive in from Vancouver is worth the trip by itself.

✨ The once-in-a-lifetime stuff
  • Peak 2 Peak GondolaWorld-record cable span between the two mountains, glass-floor cabins available, and Grandma rides it in street shoes. The view is absurd. Photos
  • Vallea LuminaA nighttime illuminated forest walk, half fairy tale, half light show. Built for exactly a 4-to-66 crowd. Photos
  • Fire & Ice showFree Sunday-night village show: pros jumping through burning rings, fireworks after. Falls inside our week. Photos
  • Scandinave SpaSilent outdoor hot-cold pools in a snowy forest. The parents' recovery day; Grandma's too. Photos
👪 For each person
  • The 4 & 6
    Magic Castle and Tree Fort kids' zones on the mountain, the Coca-Cola tube park, ski school that's babysitting with a lift ticket.
  • The 12
    Real lessons, terrain parks, tubing, and bragging rights forever.
  • The 18
    World-class riding, ziplines through old-growth forest, the best après scene in North America.
  • Parents
    Heli-ski or cat-ski day if they want it, Bearfoot Bistro's ice room, ski-in ski-out everything.
  • Grandma
    Gondola sightseeing without skis, the flat pedestrian village, Audain Art Museum, spa pools, and everyone comes home to her for dinner.
❤️ Grandma factor: the village is flat, car-free, and lively all day; the gondola gives her the summit without the climb. Village elevation is barely above Hill Country.
⚠️ The catch: Christmas week at Whistler is peak-of-peak: it's the priciest pick in Canada and lift lines are real. Book lodging the week we decide or pay dearly.

3
Best family ski village • Canada

Mont Tremblant

Quebec • village base 875 ft
Elevation: trivialBest kid ski school in the EastNonstop IAH to Montreal ~4 hrs + 1.5 hr driveSnow: near-guaranteed

A pastel European-style village at the base of the mountain, fully pedestrian, with the gondola right in the middle of it. Smaller mountain than Whistler, but for a family with a 4 and 6 year old, that's a feature: everything is ten minutes from everything, and the ski school is famous for a reason. And it holds the single best family New Year's Eve on this list.

✨ The once-in-a-lifetime stuff
  • New Year's Eve at TremblantTorchlight descent down the mountain, then fireworks over the village with everyone out in the square. We would be standing in it together at midnight. Photos
  • Dog sledding in the LaurentiansReal mushing through the forest, kid-friendly outfitters, and the 6 year old gets to hug the dogs after. Photos
  • Aquaclub La SourceAn indoor waterpark built like a lake in the forest, for the day little legs are done skiing. Photos
  • The panoramic gondolaVillage square to summit views, no skis required, Grandma's daily ride. Photos
👪 For each person
  • The 4 & 6
    The kid ski school, tubing, skating in the village square, waterpark day, fireworks at midnight.
  • The 12
    Full mountain access, ziplines, tubing, village arcade freedom.
  • The 18
    Après bars, the casino (18+ in Quebec), snowmobile expeditions.
  • Parents
    Ski-in lodges, French-Canadian dining, Scandinave spa, and a village where the kids can roam safe.
  • Grandma
    Pedestrian village, gondola views, riverside Nordic baths, sleigh rides, and the fireworks from the square.
❤️ Grandma factor: 875 feet, no driving all week, gondola and sleigh instead of stairs and trails. Second only to Quebec City for her.
⚠️ The catch: it's a smaller mountain, and serious skiers notice by day three. Quebec December cold applies here too.

The Canada Double • the once-in-a-lifetime play

Tremblant + Quebec City, one trip

Here's the move nobody's talking about yet: ranks 1 and 3 sit two and a half hours apart. Fly Houston to Montreal on the 26th, ski Tremblant four nights (Dec 26 to 30) with dog sledding and the waterpark, then roll east and spend New Year's inside the walled city (Dec 30 to Jan 2): toboggan, ice hotel, Petit Champlain, midnight in Old Quebec. One country, one flight, two completely different once-in-a-lifetime weeks stitched together.

Skiing AND the Christmas-card cityBoth stops under 900 ftOne nonstop flight + easy drives
❤️ Grandma factor: she gets the village-and-gondola half, then the carriage-and-Château half. If the goal is a trip she comes home bragging about, this is it.
⚠️ The catch: two lodging bookings and a mid-trip travel day, and peak-week pricing at both stops. Worth it, but it books like two trips.

The Contenders

Every one of these is a great trip. They rank behind Canada on flights, logistics, or fit, not on magic. If the family vote pulls hard toward one of these, nobody's mad.

4
The kid-magic king • Finland

Rovaniemi, Lapland

Arctic Circle • 350 ft
Elevation: noneUnbeatable for the 4 & 615 to 18 hrs, 2 stopsSnow + aurora

The honest truth: for a 4 and 6 year old, nothing on earth beats this, and it ranked first before the Canada tiebreaker. The real Santa Claus Village on the Arctic Circle, reindeer sleighs, husky teams, and northern lights from a glass igloo. It drops to fourth on one thing only: getting nine Texans there and back is a 15-hour, two-stop expedition each way.

The powder pilgrimage • Japan

Niseko, Hokkaido

Japan • village base ~1,000 ft
Elevation: easyBest snow on the planet16+ hrs of flying

The deepest, lightest, most reliable powder anywhere, under the Mt. Fuji-lookalike cone of Mt. Yotei, with steaming onsen baths at the end of every day and food that ruins you for ski-lodge chili forever. It's the adults' and teens' dream pick; it ranks fifth because it's the family's biggest logistical swing.

✨ The once-in-a-lifetime stuff
  • Waist-deep powder daysHokkaido gets nearly 50 feet of snow a season. The 18 year old will never shut up about it, correctly. Photos
  • Onsen cultureOutdoor hot springs in the snow. Grandma's joints and lungs both send thank-you notes. Photos
  • Japan itselfVending-machine hot cocoa, conveyor sushi, a Tokyo stopover on the way home. The culture is half the trip. Photos
❤️ Grandma factor: onsens are genuinely great for her, and the village is low. But 16-plus hours of flying each way is a real ask at 66.
⚠️ The catch: the longest journey on the list, jet lag with little kids, and Christmas-week Niseko prices like Aspen. A bucket-list trip that may fit better when the littles are bigger.

The dark horse • USA

Whitefish, Montana

Montana • town 3,033 ft
Town elevation: fineNo passports needed1-stop, ~6 to 7 hrsBudget-friendliest ski pick

A real Western mountain town, not a built resort: one walkable Main Street, a legit ski hill above it, Glacier National Park at the edge of town, and half the crowds and prices of anywhere in Colorado. If passports become the bottleneck or the budget vote wins, this is the answer.

✨ The good stuff
  • Sleigh-ride dinnersHorse-drawn sleigh to a backcountry lodge dinner. All nine of us in one sleigh. Photos
  • Glacier's edge in winterFrozen Lake McDonald and snow-ghost forests by car, no hiking required. Photos
  • Skiing among the snow ghostsWhitefish Mountain's frozen-tree summit runs are famous, and the family ski school is strong. Photos
❤️ Grandma factor: town sits at 3,033 feet, well under half of Granby, with flat shopping and a lakeside spa. One honest note: the ski summit is 6,817, so she stays in town or mid-mountain and skips the top.
⚠️ The catch: it's the least "once in a lifetime" of the ten, and that summit number means one corner of the trip isn't for her.

The beautiful asterisk • Canada

Banff & Lake Louise

Alberta • Banff 4,540 ft, Lake Louise 5,200 ft
The one elevation gambleMaybe the prettiest place in CanadaNonstop IAH to Calgary ~4.5 hrs

Here's where I broke from the original deck, which had this eighth: I dropped it to ninth on purpose. Banff might be the most beautiful mountain scenery on the continent, it's Canadian, and the flights are easy. But it's the only destination on this list that gambles with the one veto that ends the whole trip. Banff sits at 4,540 feet and Lake Louise at 5,200: on paper 40 percent below Granby, probably fine, but Grandma drew a hard line, and a trip built to win her back shouldn't open with "probably." If her doctor clears it enthusiastically someday, this jumps to the top three instantly. Until then, Canada has two better answers with zero asterisks.

❤️ Grandma factor: hot springs and gondolas she'd love, at the one elevation on the list that echoes the trip she hated. Her doctor's call, not ours.
⚠️ The catch: the elevation IS the catch. Everything else is world-class.

10
The aurora gamble • Norway

Tromsø

Northern Norway • sea level
Elevation: zeroBest northern lights odds on earth2 to 3 hrs of twilight per day

The northern lights capital of the world, and at sea level Grandma breathes perfectly. It ranks last for one honest reason: in late December the sun does not rise here at all, just 2 to 3 hours of blue twilight. Some adults find that magical. A 4 and 6 year old eating dinner in full darkness at 4pm for a week is a different story. And if we want the aurora with daylight and Santa attached, Rovaniemi already does that at rank 4.

❤️ Grandma factor: heated aurora boats and buses, flat indoor aquarium, cable-car views. Physically the easiest trip here; the darkness is the only question.
⚠️ The catch: polar night. Plus 16-plus hours of flying, and the aurora never signs a contract.

The final call

Top picks by what matters most

  • 🏆 Best for everyone: Quebec CitySea level, walkable, magic for the littles, photo-perfect for the 18 year old, fine dining for the parents, effortless for Grandma. The Christmas card, in person.
  • 🏀 Best if we want to ski: WhistlerThe best low-altitude ski week on earth. Separate kid, teen, adult, and Grandma scenes that all meet for dinner.
  • 🆘 Best of both: The Canada DoubleTremblant's slopes and fireworks, then New Year's inside the walls of Old Quebec. The once-in-a-lifetime version of a Canada trip.
  • 🎅 The wildcard that beats them all for the littles: RovaniemiIf the family decides the 4 and 6 year old outrank the flight time, Santa on the Arctic Circle wins and everyone knows it.
  • 💰 The budget dark horse: WhitefishNo passports, easiest logistics, real snow, half the price. Nothing wrong with it but the wow ceiling.
