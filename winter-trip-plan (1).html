Family Trip Planning • Christmas to New Year

Quebec City: The Trip

Decided: eight nights inside the walled city, December 26 to January 3. Ski mornings for the crews that ski, slow mornings for the crew that does not, and everybody back together every afternoon. This page is the whole plan.

Dec 26 to Jan 3 • 8 nights NYE inside the walls 9 travelers, ages 4 to 66 ~$25K of a $30K ceiling
The system

Two tracks in the morning, one family by afternoon

Nine people, three speeds: the ski crew (the 4 and 6 year old's family, out 2 to 3 mornings), the city crew (the 12 and 18 year old's family, skiing once), and Grandma, who runs best after noon. The whole week runs on one rhythm so nobody waits on anybody and nobody misses anything that matters.

The daily rhythm

⛷️ Ski mornings

Out the door by 7:45, on the mountain for first lifts, ski hard 4 hours, rolling home by 1:00. Skiing is knocked out before the city even finishes coffee.

☕ Slow mornings

No alarms. Loft breakfast, the market square outside the door, a wander window from about 10 to 12:30. Windows, not schedules; see something, chase it.

🤝 Together from 1:30

Everyone converges back at the lofts around 1:30, Grandma fresh and ready (her day starts after 12), and the afternoon and evening are one family: falls, toboggans, skating, dinners, fireworks.

❤️ The Grandma rule, baked in: nothing she's part of starts before noon. Mornings are hers: slow coffee, the square from the window. Every afternoon anchor below (carriage, falls, tea, skating benches, dinners, fireworks) is built flat, warm-up-friendly, and after 12.
Meetup default: when in doubt, everyone's back at the lofts at 1:30. One address, one time, all week. Change it by group text, not by plan.
Getting there and around

Flights, vans, and the ski shuttle question

  • ✈️ Fly Houston to Quebec City, one stopIAH to YQB with one connection (Montreal, Toronto, or a US hub), roughly 7 to 9 hours door to door. Book the morning departure so we land with daylight left. The alternative, nonstop to Montreal plus a 3 hour drive, only wins if fares gap by $150+ a seat; with 9 people and luggage, the extra flight leg beats the extra drive.
  • 🚗 Two minivans for the week, and here's whyRideshare covers the old city fine, but we leave it five times: airport twice, the mountain 2 to 3 times, Valcartier, and the falls. Two minivans at the airport solve all of it, and Quebec law requires winter tires on rentals December through March, so they come properly shod. Park in the underground garage below our square and forget them on city days.
  • 🏙️ The old city itself needs zero drivingOnce parked, everything inside the walls is a walk: the toboggan, the funicular, Petit Champlain, skating, the market, every restaurant on our list. The vans are for mountains, not streets.
The ski operation

Mont-Sainte-Anne, done right

★ 4.5 (3,799)30 min east on Route 1382,050 ft vertical, 71 trailsBase ~575 ft, zero altitude issue

Our mountain all week: a real 2,050-foot-vertical mountain with a base-to-summit gondola, a strong ski school, and on-mountain daycare right by the gondola. Big enough that the adults never get bored, close enough that a half-day is genuinely a half-day.

  • Book before we fly (the one hard rule)Christmas-week ski school for the 4 and 6 year old, plus reserved rental gear for everyone skiing, gets booked online the same week we book flights. Holiday lessons sell out; walk-ups get scraps.
  • First ski morning runs 45 minutes heavierBoot fitting and rental pickup for the crew takes real time once. Leave the lofts at 7:30 that first day; every ski morning after, gear's dialed and it's 7:45 out, skiing by 9 (8:30 on weekends), done by 12:30.
  • How a joint ski day worksOn the one morning both families ski: littles into ski school at 9, the 12 and 18 year old ride with the adults, everyone converges for a summit-lodge hot chocolate at 11:30, last runs, home by 1:30. Grandma's option that morning: ride out with us and take the gondola to the summit lodge in street shoes, watch the St. Lawrence with a coffee, or keep her slow city morning. Her call, standing offer.
  • Montmorency Falls is on the way homeThe frozen waterfall taller than Niagara sits right on Route 138 between the mountain and the city. We stack it onto a ski-day afternoon so the drive earns double.

One honest flag: the ice hotel calendar

The Hôtel de Glace is rebuilt from scratch every winter and typically opens in the first days of January. Some years that lands inside our window (the Jan 2 weekend), some years it's days after we fly home; the grounds and ice bar sometimes preview earlier. So it's penciled as a Saturday Jan 2 possibility, confirmed when dates publish in the fall, and the Valcartier tubing mountain next door runs all week either way. If the hotel opens while we're there, we're walking through it. If not, the tubing day alone is worth the drive.

Dec 26 to Jan 3 • windows, not schedules

Day by Day

Times below are meeting anchors and loose windows. Inside a window, wander: that's the point of basing inside the walls. The only fixed things all week are lift tickets, dinner reservations, and midnight on the 31st.

Sat Dec 26 • Travel day

Christmas was yesterday at home, like always. Morning flight IAH to Quebec City, land mid-afternoon, grab the two vans, 25 minutes to the lofts. First walk under the lights before dinner because nobody will be able to wait. Easy dinner two doors down at La Buchette, early night.

🤝 Everyone

Land, settle, one lap of the square, casual dinner, sleep. Grandma's only job: pick her window seat in the loft.

Sun Dec 27 • The old city, together

No skis, no vans, no splitting up. This is the day the city sells itself.

☕ Morning window, 9:30 to 12

Big breakfast at La Bûche (the maple sugar-shack feast), then wander: funicular down to Petit Champlain, Place Royale, the shops still dressed for Christmas.

🤝 From noon

Grandma joins for lunch. Afternoon: first runs on the 1884 toboggan by the Château (littles ride with a parent), hot chocolate, and a dusk carriage ride through the walls for Grandma with whoever calls shotgun. Dinner at Le Lapin Sauté, booked ahead, fireplace on.

Mon Dec 28 • Ski Day 1 (ski crew) + museum morning (city crew)

⛷️ Ski crew, 7:30 to 1:30

Heavy morning: out at 7:30 for rental fitting, littles into ski school at 9, parents ski the real mountain, done 12:30, home by 1:30. Hardest wake-up of the week, and it buys two more easy ones.

☕ City crew window, 10 to 12:30

Slow start, then the Musée de la civilisation (the teens' pick) or Saint-Jean street shopping. Grandma: coffee, the square, zero agenda.

🤝 From 1:30

Regroup at the lofts, then skating at Place D'Youville right outside the walls: skaters skate, benchers hold hot chocolate, Grandma judges form. Casual dinner at Savini on our street.

Tue Dec 29 • Tubing mountain day

🛣️ Both families, 10 to 2:30

Valcartier Winter Playground, 25 minutes out: 35+ tubing lanes and a lift tows the tube uphill, so the 4 year old and the 18 year old ride the same mountain all day. Lunch on site.

☕ Grandma's morning

Her slowest day: the lofts, the market square, maybe the Château lobby with a book. Option: ride out at noon to watch the last tubing hour and join lunch.

🤝 From 3:30

Home, thaw, then the big cook-in: one pot of something Québécois in the loft kitchen, laundry running, kids in pajamas by 8. Every trip needs this night.

Wed Dec 30 • Ski Day 2, the joint one + falls afternoon

⛷️ Everyone who skis, 7:45 to 1:30

The all-ski morning: littles in school, the 12 and 18 year old riding with all four parents, summit-lodge hot chocolate summit at 11:30, last runs, home 1:30. Gear's already fitted, so it's a clean 7:45 out.

☕ Grandma's morning

Slow start, then her standing offer: gondola to the summit lodge in street shoes to meet the hot-chocolate summit at 11:30, or stay city-side and meet us at 2.

🤝 From 2

Montmorency Falls on the drive home: cable car up, the frozen sugarloaf cone, family photo, one hour. Evening split on purpose: the four parents take the splurge table at Chez Rioux & Pettigrew at 7:30 while the 18 year old runs pizza-and-movie night at the lofts for real babysitting money.

Thu Dec 31 • New Year's Eve

☕ All morning: nothing

Sleep in. Late waffles. The only scheduled item before noon is nobody scheduling anything.

🤝 From noon

Last toboggan runs and Petit Champlain souvenirs in the afternoon, early family dinner at 5:30, littles nap 7 to 9:30 if they'll take it. Then the walled city turns into one outdoor party: music, lights, fireworks at midnight. We walk out the door, stand in it together, and walk home. Grandma's choice: the square at midnight or the show from the loft window with the kettle on. Both count.

Fri Jan 1 • The soft day

☕ Recovery morning

City sleeps, we sleep. Brunch at Cochon Dingue around 11 when it opens up.

🤝 Afternoon, unhurried

The unplanned afternoon on purpose: the thing everyone's been asking to go back to, they get to go back to. Skating, one more chute run, a café crawl, naps. Easy dinner, early night; two crews have a morning tomorrow.

Sat Jan 2 • Ski Day 3 (optional) + the ice hotel window

⛷️ Ski crew, 7:15 to 1

The bonus morning if legs vote yes: Saturday lifts spin at 8:30, ski till noon, home by 1. Skip it guilt-free if the 31st ran long; the tickets are the only loss.

☕ City crew window, 10 to 12:30

Old-city farewell lap: the market, last pastries, the photo everyone forgot to take.

🤝 From 1:30

If the Hôtel de Glace opened this weekend (confirmed in the fall), this is its slot: 2 to 4:30, ice chandeliers, ice slide, drinks in ice glasses. If not, Strøm spa hour for Grandma and the moms while the dads run the kids one last time down the toboggan. Big farewell dinner at La Bûche, the loud maple one, on purpose.

Sun Jan 3 • Home

Pack, one last square lap, midday flight, land Houston, drive to Livingston by evening. The buffer day did its job: nobody's walking into Monday off a red-eye.

The pull-in option, if we want it: everything above survives shifting to a Sat Jan 2 departure; Ski Day 3 and the ice-hotel window are the two things that fall off. Decide when we book flights, not now.

Bottom line

Eight nights, New Year's inside the walls, real skiing for the family that skis, a real city for the family that doesn't, Grandma's rhythm respected every single day, and about $4,600 of air under the ceiling. The booking order when the family says go: flights, lofts, ski school, the three dinner tables. I'd make those calls in one afternoon.
